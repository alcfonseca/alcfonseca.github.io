Collaborating Across Teams to Integrate SAP Companion ⸺

UX

User Testing

2022

My Role. I concentrated on UX and collaborated closely with two team members. Additionally, I worked with colleagues from both the broader SAP Build team and the SAP Companion team, which included developers, product managers, integration developers, and user assistance developers.

Goals. I worked with different teams and roles to integrate SAP's in-app help software into our product, SAP Build Process Automation. The goal was to establish a centralized hub for all users, whether casual or power users, to easily access extra information about features and processes.

Target Users. This feature targets Casual and Power users. Called Citizen Developers, this role has low to no code experience but high business process knowledge.

Initial Preparations ⸺

SAP Companion. First, we searched for existing solutions within the company and came across SAP Companion, an embedded application that offers in-context help and was recommended for adoption. As the lead, I organized and facilitated meetings with project managers and developers from both our team and SAP Companion's team to integrate the solution.

Current Solutions. While integrating the SAP Companion, we conducted workshops to analyze the pros and cons of our current help options.

Figuring it Out ⸺

Problem Shaping Workshop. As part of an internal quality sprint, we held a workshop with key stakeholders to identify current issues, and used the data to conduct problem solution mappings and clustering, resulting in user journeys. Using these findings, we created user flows to identify overlapping and missing solutions.

User Journey. From the outset, our goal was to reduce the number of help offerings and centralize them in SAP Companion as the primary help feature. This approach was informed by the results of the workshop and subsequent analysis, which helped us identify areas for improvement and refine our strategy for integrating SAP Companion.

Results ⸺

User Testing. Once we had an idea on how it would work, we conducted internal testing with experts. The results showed that all users were able to use the feature effectively, as confirmed by the recordings. Additionally, users found value in the guidance functionalities they utilized, which was reflected in their responses to post-task questionnaires. Areas for improvement were shared with the UX team of the SAP Companion.

Handover. With the outcome of the user testing and some additional modifications, we have successfully implemented the base features and plan to fully integrate SAP Companion by the end of 2023. With help from User Assistance, the SAP Companion is now being populated frequently with new content.
