Uniting products in Global Settings ⸺

UX/UI

2021

Product. SAP Process Automation is SAP's low-code no-code hyperautomation solution, that joins automated bots with workflow management to create an end-to-end solution for automating processes.

Goals and Challenges. With the creation of SAP Process Automation, many different product areas were joined into one central product. The problem was that each of these areas had different settings, and so the objective was to join all the different configurations into one area. This would provide value by offering easy access to all settings in a central place for our personas, the IT Admin and Citizen Developer.

Initial Preparations ⸺

Requirements. The first step was to sync with the responsible Project Manager and discuss requirements and scope. This then led to further talks with each product area's stakeholders to decide on what had to be included into the Global Settings. We also decided that the solution had to be flexible enough to allow each area to add their own content when needed.

Content Analysis. To figure out how to structure the final solution, we set out analysing the current state of the settings in each product area that would be added.

Benchmarking. We didn't want to go far from SAP's own solutions for settings but knew we wanted to provide a great experience. For this, we benchmarked internal SAP products like the internal wiki and Fiori Launchpad as well as the Fiori and BTP guidelines. Apart from that we also looked at external sites, like Google, Github and Jira.

Figuring it Out ⸺

Wireframes. After the groundwork was laid out, we went onto creating initial explorations in Figma. Here we experimented with different forms of navigation and layout.

Iterations. After various feedback sessions with stakeholders and UX colleagues we moved on to high-fidelity prototypes, where we could iron out the details of the concept.

Results ⸺

The result is a flexible Global Settings page where each product area can add what configurations they see fit. The sidebar allows for grouping of different inner pages and the main content area provides flexibility for various content types while still following SAP's Fiori and Business Technology Platform UX guidelines.
