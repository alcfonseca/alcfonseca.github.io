Simplifying the Modeling Experience ⸺

Product Design

User Testing

2023

My Role. Together with my colleague Diego, I was UX lead for this initiative. We teamed up with other UX colleagues and closely collaborated with product managers, developers, and researchers within our product to ensure the success of the redesign.

Goals. Following an extensive user study of our product's main user journey, we identified numerous opportunities for improvement and usability issues. Our goal was to enhance the modeling experience by addressing these issues.

Target Users. This feature targets Casual and Power users, referred to as Citizen Developers, who have low to no code experience but high business process knowledge.

Preliminary User Study ⸺

Testing. The research team tested the product's main user journey in a live environment using participants recruited through Respondent. This approach enabled them to select the appropriate user type that aligns with our target persona.

Results. We identified 10 critical usability themes that needed attention, with the top 3 concerns being: a confusing overview and difficulty in modeling a process, misunderstanding error icons as delete actions, and terminology issues, particularly within the adding menu.

Old design

Figuring it Out ⸺

Workshop. The UX team, developers, and product managers convened for a 3-day creative workshop in Berlin to collaboratively address the top usability issues. During these days, we thoroughly discussed the study findings, addressed each usability theme, brainstormed potential solutions, prioritized solutions, and outlined the next steps.

Mockups. Following the workshop, the UX team compiled the data and developed low-fidelity mockups. These were employed to gather preliminary feedback from internal stakeholders. Our new designs prioritize a guided modeling experience to support our target users, incorporating natural language for improved comprehension. The adding menu is now more discoverable and scalable, and the modeling experience is now made in such a way to prevent errors in processes.

User Studies ⸺

Testing. Armed with high-fidelity prototypes, we conducted an extensive array of studies. The initial study involved participants recruited through Respondent who had no prior knowledge of the product. Subsequently, we carried out a series of diverse studies using the new design within the live system. These sessions encompassed Beta customers who had already embraced the new design, big customers unfamiliar with the new designs, and partners at one of our SAP events.

Results. We received a ton of positive feedback from our participants and customers and saw an improvement in our editor's SUS and NPS scores by 20 points. It was a great marriage between our usability improvements, the new Fiori Horizon theming, and the new SAP brand identity, which creates a really strong story for selling this to the customer.

Results and Next Steps ⸺

New Designs. The updated concept adopts a vertical view as default alongside the classic BPMN horizontal layout. This adjustment enables us to accommodate more content within the canvas, aligning with our natural inclination to scroll horizontally. The revised block layout facilitates user identification of process groups, avoiding the confusion of tangled lines and nodes. Additionally, the inclusion of natural language and a guided modeling experience enhances usability, catering perfectly to our target audience.

Next Steps. To continually enhance our product, we've implemented an in-product survey to gather ongoing feedback from our customers. Additionally, we have a series of smaller improvements in the pipeline, scheduled for release in the near future.
